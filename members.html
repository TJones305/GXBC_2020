Bowls Basics

The objective of the game of bowls in it's most basic form is for you and your teams bowls ( or woods) to be closer to the jack (the small yellow or white target ball) than your opposition. For each bowl closer to the jack than your opposition, your team scores 1 point. For example, both teams have a total of 8 bowls each. Your team has 3 bowls closer than your opponent's nearest bowl to the jack, therfore your team scores 3 points. There is more complexity to the game as you develop your skill and experience, but the above highlights scoring in it's simplest form.

Controlled Weight

This shot is used when wanting to slightly move the jack's position or an opponent's bowl, depending on the situation.

Play Video
Run Shot

This is a more aggressive version of a controlled weight shot, which aims to open tightly packed heads (group of bowls around the jack).

Play Video
Driving (aka Firing)

This shot is a rarity, but normally used in highly disadvantageous situations. It involves playing the bowl with a great deal of force that anything it hits will also be cleared out the way.
